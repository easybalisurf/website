A quick guide to Bali's waves

Everything on the Live Forecast panel — wave size, wind, swell, tide — comes from this short primer. Skim it once and the numbers on the booking page will actually mean something.

Wave size & who it suits

Knee–waist high (0.3–0.8m) — first timers & kids. Whitewater and small, slow rollers. Easiest to stand up, safest to fall.
Waist–chest high (0.8–1.4m) — improvers & intermediates. Enough push to start turning and reading sections of a wave.
Head high and up (1.4m+) — advanced riders. Faster, more powerful reef and point breaks — Uluwatu on a good day lives here.

Wind: why "offshore" is the magic word

Wind blowing from land out to sea (offshore) grooms the wave face smooth and holds it up — the best conditions of the day. Wind blowing along the coast (cross-shore) is still rideable but choppier. Wind blowing straight into the wave from the ocean (onshore) makes it mushy and messy — most spots are best avoided then. Bali's trade winds are seasonal and predictable, which is why the same spot can be glassy in the morning and blown out by 1pm.

Swell, in plain terms

A "swell" is a group of waves generated by a storm far out in the ocean, which then travels — sometimes thousands of kilometers — until it reaches the coast. Swell has a height (how big) and a period (seconds between waves — longer period means more powerful, more organized waves). Our forecast reads both, plus tide, to score every time window on the booking page from "Fair" to "Prime."

Bali's main spots, at a glance

Canggu (Batu Bolong, Old Man's)
Beginner-friendly beach breaks, softest sand bottoms.
Kuta & Legian
Wide, gentle sandy beach — Bali's classic learner zone.
Uluwatu
World-class left reef break — powerful, for experienced surfers.
Balangan & Bingin
Reef breaks on the Bukit peninsula, mid to advanced.
Sanur
Sheltered lagoon — Bali's kitesurf & wing foil home base.
Nusa Lembongan
Short boat ride from Bali, punchy reef waves, fewer crowds.

Seasonality

Dry season (April–October) — consistent offshore trade winds, bigger swells on the west/southwest-facing coasts (Uluwatu, Bingin, Canggu). Best window for surfing overall, and prime time for wind-powered sports like kitesurfing and wing foiling.

Wet season (November–March) — lighter, more variable wind and smaller swell on the west coast, but this is when east-facing spots (Sanur, Nusa Lembongan) come alive. Warm water year-round either way — this is the tropics.
